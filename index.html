    How to stay safe

    Keep these steps close whenever a job offer arrives through social media, chat groups, or direct messages.

    • ✓Confirm the employer or agency through official websites, verified social pages, or trusted directories before replying.
    • ✓Pause if you are asked for money, registration fees, transport fees, visa fees, or medical fees before a formal offer.
    • ✓Protect your ID, passport, certificates, and banking details until you have verified who is requesting them and why.
    • ✓Save screenshots, names, and payment details if something feels wrong so you can report it quickly.

    Current scam warnings

    These warning signs are commonly reported in fraudulent recruitment messages targeting job seekers in Kenya.

    • !Upfront requests for application, processing, visa, training, or clearance fees.
    • !Pressure to move the conversation to WhatsApp or Telegram immediately instead of official channels.
    • !Promises of very high pay, overseas travel, or guaranteed placement with little or no interview process.
    • !Urgent requests for passport copies, ID photos, M-Pesa details, or bank information before verification.
